COVID rules

The covid rules are now much more lenient compared to the beginning of the pandemic. However, we recommend to limit the contacts in the days prior to the event in order to protect those more vulnerable.

  • During the event masking is not mandatory but it can be done, of course.
  • No test or proof of vaccionation must be presented to participate in the event.
  • In the bars and restaurants masking is also not mandatory.
  • Masking is mandatory in all public transportation but not in the stops, train stations and airports.
  • Vaccination proof or tests are not mandatory for entering or leaving the country.

Pamplona · Iruña

Pamplona is a city full of life and with plenty stuff to see. Here you have a list of some places you should not miss if you have some free time in Pamplona. All these places are close to the city center and can be visited on foot. You can also take a look at the official website.

  • The walls
  • Taconera park
  • Ciudadela de Pamplona (defensive fortress)
  • Plaza del castillo (main square)
  • Estafeta street (part of the bullrunning route + pintxos)
  • Santa María la Real Cathedral

In Pamplona there are also many bars and restaurants where you can drink something, eat some pintxos or enjoy a meal. We also recommend you try once a "Sidrería" (they should serve a menu with chorizo with cider, cod omelette, veal rib steak (chuletón) and cheese with quince; all paired with natural cider).

  • Iruñazarra (pintxos and restaurant)
  • Melbourne (restaurant)
  • Casa Manolo (restaurant)
  • Erretegia (grill)
  • Auzmendi (sidrería)
  • Txirrintxa (bar & grill)
  • Bodegón Sarría (pintxos)
  • Zanpa (pintxos)
  • Mandarra de la Ramos (pintxos and restaurant)
  • Casa Otano (pintxos and restaurant)
  • La mejillonera (bar)
  • Mesón de la Nabarrería (bar)
